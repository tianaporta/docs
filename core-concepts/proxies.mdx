## What are Proxies?

Proxies route Halo's requests through different IP addresses so each task looks like it's coming from a separate user. Without proxies, all your tasks hit a site from the same IP, which gets flagged fast on competitive drops.

For most modules, proxies aren't strictly required on small task counts — but on any serious drop, you want them.

## Proxy Types

**ISP** — residential IPs hosted on datacenter infrastructure. Fast and clean. Good default for most sites.

**Residential** — real home/mobile IPs. Most authentic but usually slower and more expensive. Required for the hardest sites (Shopify hyped drops, Footsites, PKC queue).

**Datacenter** — cheapest but most easily detected. Fine for Amazon, gets banned fast on Shopify and Footsites.

## Adding Proxies

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Proxies tab">
    Head to the **Proxies** tab in the top nav.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a group">
    Click the **+** next to **Groups**. Name it after the proxy provider or use case (e.g. `Residential 1`, `ISP US`, `Footsites Pack`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add proxies">
    Click **Add Proxies** at the bottom. Paste your proxies, one per line.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Proxy Format

Halo accepts proxies in either format, one per line:
ip:port
ip:port:username:password

Examples:
192.168.1.1:8080
192.168.1.2:8080:user:pass

Most paid proxies use the `ip:port:username:password` format. Free or self-hosted proxies are usually just `ip:port`.

## Choosing the Right Proxy

| Site / Module | Recommended Proxy |
|---|---|
| Amazon | ISP or Datacenter |
| Pokémon Center | Residential (ISP rarely passes queue) |
| Best Buy | ISP or Residential |
| Footsites | Residential |
| Shopify (hyped) | Residential |
| Shopify (standard) | ISP |
| Funko / PopMart | ISP or Residential |
| EQL | ISP or Residential |

## Tips

- **Don't mix proxy types in the same group.** Keep ISP separate from residential.
- **Match proxy region to site region.** US proxies for US sites, EU for EU sites.
- **Rotate providers.** Bad proxies = banned tasks; spreading risk across providers helps.
- **Test before drops.** Run a low-stakes task to confirm your proxies work before a big release.
- **More proxies = more spread.** Higher task counts need a bigger proxy pool to avoid IP overlap.

## Managing Proxies

- **Import / Export** — bulk move proxies in and out
- **Search** — find specific proxies inside a group
- **Delete** — remove selected proxies
- Multiple groups let you assign different proxy types to different modules
