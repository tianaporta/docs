## What are Accounts?

Accounts are the logins Halo uses to checkout on sites that require authentication. Sites like Best Buy, Funko, EQL, PopMart, and Amazon all need accounts — you'll get a `(Required)` flag in the task setup when one is needed.

Accounts in Halo are grouped by site, so all your Best Buy accounts live in one group, all your Funko accounts in another, and so on.

## Adding Accounts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Accounts tab">
    Head to the **Accounts** tab in the top nav.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a group">
    Click the **+** next to **Groups**. Name your group (e.g. `BestBuy 1`, `Funko EU`) and pick the **Account Type** from the dropdown — this needs to match the site you'll use the accounts on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add accounts">
    Click **+ New Account** at the bottom. Paste your accounts in the format `email:password`, one per line. Then click **Add Accounts**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Account types are site-specific. A PopMart account group can only be used on PopMart tasks, a Funko group on Funko tasks, etc.
</Note>

## Managing Accounts

- **Import / Export** — bulk move accounts in and out using the buttons in the top right
- **Search** — find specific accounts inside a group
- **Actions** — bulk actions like delete or move between groups
- **Delete** — remove selected accounts

## Account Format

Halo expects accounts in this format, one per line:
account@example.com:password
account2@example.com:password2
account3@example.com:password3

## Tips

- **Match account region to site region.** A PopMartCA account won't work on PopMartUS, etc.
- **Aged accounts perform better** than fresh ones on raffles and limited drops
- **Keep groups organized by site** — don't mix BestBuy and Funko accounts in one group
- **Quality > quantity** on raffle drops; flagged accounts won't checkout no matter how many tasks you run
