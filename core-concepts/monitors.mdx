## What are Monitors?

Monitors watch a site (or a specific product) in the background and trigger tasks the moment something changes — a restock, a new product, a price drop. Instead of manually creating tasks the second a drop happens, monitors keep you ahead of the release.

Pair them with **Quick Task Preset** to auto-spawn tasks when the monitor detects something, and **Live Feed** to see what monitors are picking up in real time.

## Creating a Monitor Group

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Monitors tab">
    Head to the **Monitors** tab in the top nav.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a group">
    Click the **+** next to **Groups**. Name it (e.g. `PKC Restock Watch`, `Shopify Drops`) and pick the **Monitor Type** from the dropdown — this matches the site you want to monitor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Confirm">
    Your group is ready. Now add monitors to it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Creating a Monitor

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click + New Monitor">
    From inside your monitor group, hit **+ New Monitor** at the bottom.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in monitor details">
    - **Monitor Name** — internal name (e.g. `Charizard ex Restock`)
    - **Site** — the site to watch (or `ALL` to monitor across all sites in the group's type)
    - **Mode** — Normal (Safe Preload), Safe (Password page), Fast (Speed), Super Safe (Browser add to cart), or App (Mobile)
    - **Proxy Group** — proxies for the monitor to use
    - **Keywords/URL** — the keyword or product URL to watch for
    - **Sizes** — specific sizes or `Random`
    - **Profiles** — profile(s) to attach
    - **Tasks Count** — how many tasks to auto-spawn when the monitor triggers
    - **Quantity** — units per spawned task
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Monitor">
    The monitor is added to your group. Click **Start** to begin watching.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Monitor Controls

The bottom of the Monitors page has all the controls:

- **+ New Monitor** — create a new monitor
- **Start / Stop** — start or stop selected monitors
- **Delete** — remove selected monitors
- **Edit** — change monitor settings
- **Quick Task Preset** — pre-configure how tasks should spawn when the monitor triggers (mode, profiles, accounts, etc.)
- **Live Feed** — real-time view of what your monitors are picking up
- **Set Proxies** — assign or change the proxy group for selected monitors

## Quick Task Preset

The **Quick Task Preset** is the bridge between a monitor finding something and tasks actually running. When you set up a preset, Halo knows exactly how to spawn tasks the moment the monitor fires.

Set the preset once with your preferred mode, profiles, accounts, and task count — then any monitor in that group will auto-spawn tasks matching that preset when it detects a product.

## Live Feed

The Live Feed shows everything your monitors are catching as it happens — new products, restocks, price changes, queue detection. Useful for:

- **Watching a drop unfold** in real time
- **Catching missed products** that didn't match your keyword exactly
- **Debugging** why a monitor might not be triggering tasks

## Recommended Setup

- **One monitor group per site or use case** — easier to manage than a single mega-group
- **Set Quick Task Preset before drops** so tasks spawn instantly when the monitor triggers
- **Use ALL site mode** for broad watches (e.g. "any Charizard restock across all Shopify sites")
- **Use specific site mode** for targeted watches (e.g. "PKC Charizard ex restock only")
- **Pair with Live Feed** during big drops to see what's hitting in real time
- **Match proxy quality to the site** — same proxy rules as tasks apply

## Tips

- Monitors run continuously — they don't stop until you stop them
- More monitors = more requests = more proxy usage; budget accordingly on hyped drops
- A flaky proxy will give you false negatives (missed restocks); good proxies matter
- If a monitor isn't triggering, check the Live Feed — it'll show what the monitor is actually seeing
- Quick Task Preset is the difference between a useful monitor and just a fancy alert system
