## What is IMAP?

IMAP lets Halo read verification codes and checkout links directly from your inbox during a task. Without it, drops that require email verification (BestBuy, Topps, etc.) will stall.

<Note>
You'll use an **app password** — not your regular email password. App passwords are generated by your email provider and only grant IMAP access. Your real password stays private.
</Note>

## Setup

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Gmail">
    **1. Enable 2FA**

    Go to [myaccount.google.com/signinoptions/two-step-verification/enroll-welcome](https://myaccount.google.com/signinoptions/two-step-verification/enroll-welcome) and follow the steps.

    **2. Create App Password**

    1. Go to [myaccount.google.com/apppasswords](https://myaccount.google.com/apppasswords)
    2. Name it `Halo` and click **Create**
    3. Copy the 16-character code
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Yahoo">
    1. Go to [login.yahoo.com/myaccount/security](https://login.yahoo.com/myaccount/security)
    2. Turn on **2-Step Verification**
    3. Scroll to **Other ways to sign in** → **Generate app password**
    4. Select **Other App**, name it `Halo`, hit **Generate**
    5. Copy the code
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Outlook">
    <Note>Only works if you have 2FA enabled on your Microsoft account.</Note>

    1. Go to [account.microsoft.com](https://account.microsoft.com) → **Security**
    2. Click **Advanced Security Options**
    3. Under **App Passwords**, click **Create a new app password**
    4. Name it `Halo` and copy the code
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="iCloud">
    1. Go to [appleid.apple.com](https://appleid.apple.com) and sign in
    2. Under **Security**, click **Generate Password**
    3. Label it `Halo` and hit **Create**
    4. Copy the code
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="AOL">
    1. Go to [login.aol.com/myaccount/security](https://login.aol.com/myaccount/security)
    2. Turn on **2-Step Verification**
    3. Scroll to **Generate app password**
    4. Select **Other**, name it `Halo`, hit **Generate**
    5. Copy the code
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Adding Your IMAP to Halo

Once you have your app password from your email provider, add it to Halo.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the IMAP tab">
    Head to the **Imap** tab in the top nav.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a group">
    Click the **+** next to **Groups**. Name your group (e.g. `Gmail`, `Outlook Accounts`) and confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click + New Imap">
    Hit **+ New Imap** at the bottom of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick your provider">
    Select your email provider from the dropdown — Google (Gmail), Yahoo, Outlook / Hotmail, iCloud, AOL, or Custom for other providers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste your IMAPs">
    Paste your accounts one per line in `email:password` format, using the **app password** from the setup steps above — not your regular email password.

    ```
    user@gmail.com:app-password-here
    user2@gmail.com:app-password-here
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hit Add IMAPs">
    Halo will validate and connect each IMAP. Status appears in the table — green means connected and ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
**Custom provider?** Pick **Custom** from the dropdown and use the format `host:port:email:password` (e.g. `imap.gmail.com:993:user@gmail.com:password`). The format hint at the bottom of the modal shows exactly what's expected.
</Note>

## Managing IMAPs

- **Set Proxies** — route IMAP connections through proxies if needed
- **Change Group** — move IMAPs between groups
- **Start / Stop** — manually start or stop the IMAP connection
- **Import / Export** — bulk manage IMAPs
- **Status** — shows whether the IMAP is connected, disconnected, or errored

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why isn't my IMAP submitting codes?">
    - The code was never sent. Some sites make tasks wait on codes that never arrive.
    - The code is in the wrong folder. Halo only reads the Inbox.
    - Your device time is out of sync.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I sync my device time?">
    **Windows:** Settings → Time & Language → Date & time → toggle "Set time automatically" ON → click "Sync now"

    **Mac:** System Settings → General → Date & Time → toggle "Set time and date automatically" ON
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use my regular email password?">
    No. Modern email providers require app passwords for third-party access.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
